# MaRe Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Essence
MaRe is a biodynamic Italian skincare line that combines rigorous scientific protocols with artisanal luxury. We speak to elevated professionals who value precision, efficacy, and the subtle art of understatement.

## Voice Principles

### 1. Scientific Authority Without Jargon
- Lead with results and methodology
- Reference clinical protocols, not marketing buzzwords
- Speak as a knowledgeable peer, not a salesperson

### 2. Quiet Luxury
- Let quality speak through restraint
- No excessive punctuation or enthusiasm
- Confidence expressed through precision, not volume

### 3. Italian Heritage
- Subtle references to craft and tradition
- Biodynamic farming as philosophy, not trend
- Generational expertise implied, not stated

### 4. Mutual Exclusivity
- We select partners; we don't pitch to everyone
- Standards-based partnership language
- The salon qualifies for MaRe, not the reverse

## Email Communication Standards

### Subject Lines
- Under 50 characters
- No punctuation at end
- Specific, not clever
- Examples:
  - "MaRe Eye Protocol for [Salon Name]"
  - "Retail conversion data for [City] market"
  - "Partnership inquiry for [Salon Name]"

### Body Structure
- Maximum 3 sentences
- No greeting like "Hi" or "Hello"
- Sign off: "— Rebecca"
- Direct address without familiarity

### Good Example
```
Your focus on corrective treatments at [Salon] aligns with MaRe's clinical protocols. Our Eye Contour System converts at 40%+ retail in venues matching your service model. I'd welcome a brief conversation about mutual fit.

— Rebecca
```

### Bad Example
```
Hey there! I'm so excited to reach out about an amazing opportunity for your salon! MaRe is a game-changing skincare line that I just KNOW your clients will love. Let's touch base soon!

Best,
Rebecca
```

### Key Statistics
- 3% → 40%+ retail conversion improvement
- Eye Contour System as hero product
- Protocol-based selling (systematic approach)

### Value Proposition Structure
1. Acknowledge their current excellence
2. Present MaRe as elevation, not replacement
3. Quantify the opportunity
4. Position as selective partnership

## Partnership Language

### Mutual Exclusivity Positioning
- "We partner exclusively with venues matching MaRe's standards"
- "Your [specific attribute] meets our partnership criteria"
- "Selective distribution protects both brand equity and your competitive advantage"

### Never Say
- "We'd love to work with anyone"
- "Great opportunity for you"
- "You should carry our products"
- "Let me tell you about our line"
