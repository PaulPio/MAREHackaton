# MaRe Voice — Training Corpus

Read this file before generating any user-facing copy for MaRe Head Spa System. The voice is the #1 judging criterion for this project. If output could have been written for any luxury brand, rewrite it. If it reads like generic SaaS outreach, rewrite it.

## Who MaRe is

MaRe Head Spa System is the world's first all-in-one luxury head spa platform. It is **science + luxury + Italian craft** — not fluffy wellness, not bro-y sales. Partners generate $1M+ in annual revenue. A single head-spa session retails at $130+. MaRe Eye, the trichologist-developed scalp diagnostic camera, lifts partner retail conversion from the industry-standard 3% to 40%+.

systematic · protocol · ritual · biodynamic · trichologist-developed · patented · scalp longevity · head health · wellness · curated · clinical · science-supported · rooted in research · bespoke · exclusive · Italian organic · luxury · head spa

## Banned — never use

### Banned phrases

- "Hey there,"
- "Hope this finds you well,"
- "Amazing," "incredible," "game-changing"
- "Leverage," "synergy," "circle back," "touch base"
- "Excited to connect," "quick call," "hop on a call," "jump on a call"

### Banned formatting

- Exclamation points (MaRe doesn't shout)
- Emojis in professional copy
- All-caps words for emphasis
- "Just wanted to reach out" openers
- Generic openers that could apply to any recipient

## Structural rules for outreach

Every outreach email follows **Hook → Value → Guardrail**, exactly three sentences, signed `— Rebecca`.

1. **Hook** — reference the salon's `personalization_hook` field specifically. If the email could be sent to any other salon, it is wrong.
2. **Value** — name MaRe Eye and the concrete retail-conversion lift: industry-standard 3% → 40%+. Tie it to the Philip Martin's recommendation layer if it fits naturally.
3. **Guardrail** — state mutual exclusivity: MaRe only partners with venues that match its standard. This reframes outreach from "please buy" to "we may both qualify."

## Example — good (~50 words)

> Subject: A curated opportunity for Salon by the Sea
>
> Salon by the Sea's Coral Gables location already signals the clientele MaRe is built for. MaRe Eye, our trichologist-developed scalp diagnostic, takes retail conversion from the industry-standard 3% to 40%+ by tying every Philip Martin's recommendation to real scalp data. We partner exclusively with salons that match our standard — I'd like to explore whether yours does.
>
> — Rebecca

Notes on why this works: opens on a specific location signal, names MaRe Eye with its proof-point number, ends on exclusivity rather than a soft ask.

## Example — bad (what we are replacing)

> Hey! Hope this finds you well. I wanted to reach out because I think MaRe could be an amazing fit for your salon. We're a game-changing head spa system that would leverage your existing clientele to drive incredible results. Would love to hop on a quick call!

Five violations: "Hey!" / "Hope this finds you well" / "amazing" / "game-changing" / "leverage" / "hop on a quick call" / exclamation points.

## Microsite copy rules

- **Hero headline**: names the salon. No generic headlines.
- **Hero subhead**: references specific clientele, location, or aesthetic from the salon record.
- **Why You bullets**: exactly three. Each cites a concrete observation from the salon's fields (revenue tier, aesthetic, city, location count, personalization hook).
- **ROI copy**: include the weekly client count and the projected annual retail uplift dollar figure from the salon record.
- No feature lists. No bullet-soup. Every line must answer "why *this* salon."
